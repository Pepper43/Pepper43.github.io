Local Cuisine and Food

Explore the rich and diverse flavors of Hawaii! The local cuisine is a perfect reflection of the islands’ melting pot of cultures, including Native Hawaiian, Asian, and European influences. Below are the must-try dishes you’ll encounter during your visit


Traditional Hawaiian Dishes
Poke
  • A fresh and flavorful dish made with raw fish
    (commonly ahi tuna or salmon) marinated in
    soy sauce, sesame oil, seaweed, and scallions
  • Found everywhere from local markets to fine
    dining establishments
Kalua Pig
  • A traditional Hawaiian dish prepared by slow-roasting
    pork in an underground imu (a traditional earth oven)
  • Often served at luaus with a smoky, tender flavor

Laulau
  • A classic Hawaiian dish made with pork or fish
    wrapped in taro leaves and steamed until tender
  • A comforting and traditional favorite enjoyed
    by locals and visitors alike
Loco Moco
  • A hearty and iconic Hawaiian comfort food consisting of a
    hamburger patty, rice, a fried egg, and brown gravy

Haupia
  • A sweet, coconut-based dessert with a pudding-like consistenc.
  • Commonly served at luaus and local bakeries
