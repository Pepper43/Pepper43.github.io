Volcanoes and National Parks
Hawai’i Volcanoes
National Park (Big Island)		 Haleakalā
National Park (Maui)		 Lava Fields (Big Island)
  • Witness the power of nature at Kilauea, one of the world’s most active
    volcanoes, and Mauna Loa, the largest volcano by volume.
  • Explore lava tubes, steam vents, and hiking trails across a
    unique volcanic landscape.
  • Don’t miss the Jaggar Museum for insights into the island’s geology
  • Known as the “House of the Sun,” Haleakalā offers
    breathtaking sunrise views from its summit at over 10,000 feet.
  • Experience a range of ecosystems from tropical
    rainforests to desert-like landscapes.
  • Stargazing at night is unmatched due to its clear skies.
  • Walk on the cooled remnants of historic lava flows,
    such as the 2018 Kilauea eruption sites.
  • Visit the Kalapana region for dramatic views
    of how lava has reshaped the land.
